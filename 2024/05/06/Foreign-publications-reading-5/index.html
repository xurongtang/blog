complex sentences

sentence 1

If you go down the escalator in a building on Liberty Street, in the financial district, walk past some pickleball courts and an Alamo Drafthouse movie complex that sells truffle popcorn, and then take a few turns in a maze of basement hallways, you will find yourself in a space that might convince you, with its tall stacks of dusty VHS tapes, that the nineties never ended.
如果你沿着金融区自由街一栋大楼的自动扶梯走下去，走过一些泡菜球场和出售松露爆米花的阿拉莫露天电影院，然后在迷宫般的地下室走廊里转几圈，你会发现你置身于一个空间里，里面堆满了灰尘的 VHS 录像带，可能会让你相信 90 年代从未结束。

sentence 2

That year, Youngman Kim closed the store’s main location, on St. Marks, and more than fifty thousand tapes and DVDs were hastily packed and sent to Sicily, where the mayor of Salemi, a small town with Marfaesque cultural aspirations, agreed to house them, on Kim’s condition that they would be made available to the public
那一年，Youngman Kim 关闭了位于圣马克斯的商店主店，五万多盘磁带和 DVD 被匆忙打包并发往西西里岛，那里有着马法式文化抱负的小镇萨莱米 (Salemi) 的市长同意安置这些磁带和 DVD。金的条件是它们将向公众公开。

sentence 3

The Salemi Kim’s never became fully operational, however, and, after languishing in boxes for a dozen years, the collection made its way back to New York. (The story of how this happened is chronicled in “Kim’s Video,” a new documentary by David Redmon and Ashley Sabin.) Alamo Drafthouse stored the trove, offering select titles to its customers to borrow for free.
然而，萨莱米·金 (Salemi Kim) 从未全面投入运营，在盒子里搁置了十几年之后，这些藏品又回到了纽约。 （大卫·雷德蒙 (David Redmon) 和阿什利·萨宾 (Ashley Sabin) 拍摄的新纪录片《金的视频》(Kim’s Video) 记录了这一过程的故事。）阿拉莫绘图室 (Alamo Drafthouse) 储存了这些宝藏，并为客户提供精选图书免费借阅。

sentence 4

The Alamo people, he said, “didn’t really know what they had in the boxes, and it was just this pop-up thing where they prioritized DVDs. I couldn’t have that be the thing that represents Kim’s Video.” He contacted Alamo and volunteered to cull the tapes for free.
他说，阿拉莫人“并不真正知道盒子里有什么，他们只是优先考虑 DVD 的弹出式东西。 我不能让它成为代表 Kim 视频的东西。” 他联系了阿拉莫并自愿免费删除这些磁带。

sentence 5

But for the past year and a half he’s spent most of his Fridays in the Liberty Street basement, where he goes over the Kim’s collection with a rotating cast of friends
但在过去的一年半里，他周五的大部分时间都在自由街地下室度过，在那里他与朋友们轮流浏览 Kim 的收藏品。

sentence 6

“I don’t believe in guilty pleasures,” Williams said. “Like, ‘I know it’s bad, but I love it.’ That’s a crummy way to think.” He went on. “What if ‘Emmanuelle 5’ isn’t bad? If there’s enough moments in the movie that really get you, then what else are you looking for?”
“我不相信有罪恶感的快乐，”威廉姆斯说。 “就像，‘我知道这很糟糕，但我喜欢它。’这是一种糟糕的思维方式。” 他接着说。 “如果《艾曼纽 5》不错怎么办？ 如果电影中有足够多的时刻真正让你着迷，那么你还在寻找什么？”
